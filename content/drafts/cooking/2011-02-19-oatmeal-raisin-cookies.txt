---
title: Oatmeal Raisin Cookies
---

These are likely going to be the best oatmeal raisin cookies you’ve ever had. 
The secret is toasting the nuts before mixing them into the cookie batter. This 
gives them a unique and delicious flavor. Other than that, these are you typical 
moist and delicious oatmeal raisin cookie.

##Ingredients

* 1 cup raw cashews
* 1 cup raw walnuts
* 1/2 cup unsalted butter (softened)
* 1 cup light brown sugar
* 1/2 cup sugar
* 2 eggs
* 1 teaspoon vanilla
* 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
* 1 teaspoon baking soda
* 1 teaspoon cinnamon
* 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
* 1 teaspoons salt
* 2 cups raisins
* 3 cups medium rolled oats
* (Optional) 1/3 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
* (Optional) 1/4 cup dried cranberries
* (Optional) 1/4 cup of shredded coconuts

##Instructions

* Toast the nuts – heat these in a sauce pan until lightly browned, then remove 
and rough chop
* Dry ingredients – in medium bowl sift together:
  * Add 1 1/2 cups flour
  * Add 1 teaspoon baking soda
* Wet ingredients – in a stand mixer:
  * Cream the 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  * Add 1 cup brown sugar
  * Add 1/2 cup sugar
  * Add 2 eggs, one at a time
  * Add 1 teaspoon: cinnamon, salt, baking soda, vanilla
  * Add 1/2 teaspoon: ground cloves
* Slowly mix the dry ingredients together with the wet.
* Stir in all your fixin's (raisins, oats, nuts, etc)
* Use 2 tablespoons to create balls of the batter and place them on a silpat.
* Refridgerate the batter balls for a couple hours before baking for the best
shapped cookies.
* Pre-heat oven to 350 degrees
* Bake until a toothpick comes out clean from the center of the cookies
* Let sit for ~15 min before you remove from the silpat
