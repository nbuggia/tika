  2. January 5, 1914
    A Hundred Collars north-of-boston

    Lancaster bore him – such a little town, Such a great man. It doesn’t see him often Of late years, though he keeps the old homestead And sends the children back there with their mother In summer to get fresh air and the smell of the place. He himself can’t get away from work.

  3. January 4, 1914
    The Mountain north-of-boston

    The mountain held the town as in a shadow. I saw so much before I slept there once: I noticed that I missed stars in the west, Where its black body cut into the sky. Near me it seemed: I felt it like a wall Behind which I was sheltered from a wind.

  4. January 3, 1914
    The Death of the Hired Man north-of-boston

    Mary sat musing on the lamp-flame at the table Waiting for Warren. When she heard his step, She ran on tip-toe down the darkened passage To meet him in the doorway with the news And put him on his guard. “Silas is back.” She pushed him outward with her through the door And shut it after her. “Be kind,” she said. She took the market things from Warren’s arms And set them on the porch, then drew him down To sit beside her on the wooden steps.
